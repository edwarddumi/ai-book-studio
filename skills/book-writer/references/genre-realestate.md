# Real Estate Book Guide

## Reader Profiles
Real estate books attract three distinct audiences — write for one specifically:
1. **Beginners** — first-time buyers, new investors, people considering a license
2. **Practitioners** — active agents, investors scaling their portfolio
3. **Advanced** — team leaders, developers, commercial operators

Always define your audience in the book-config.md. The advice, vocabulary, and assumed knowledge differ dramatically.

## Popular Frameworks to Anchor Chapters Around
- The deal funnel (lead → prospect → contract → close)
- The investment stack (cash flow, equity, appreciation, tax)
- The agent growth ladder (solo → team → owner)
- The buyer/seller journey (awareness → trust → transaction → referral)

## Chapter Structure (recommended for how-to real estate)
1. **Real scenario opener** — a specific deal, client, or moment
2. **The lesson it teaches**
3. **The system or process** — step by step
4. **Scripts, templates, or checklists** (readers love these — include them)
5. **What to avoid** — common costly mistakes
6. **Your next move** — one action step

## Voice Guidelines
- Be direct. Real estate readers want to know what to DO.
- Use dollar amounts and timelines. Specificity = credibility.
- DMV/local content: if writing for a regional audience, name neighborhoods, price ranges, and market dynamics they recognize
- Avoid generic advice that could apply to any market

## Must-Include Elements (non-fiction real estate)
- At least one "war story" per chapter — a real deal that went wrong or surprisingly right
- Scripts and dialogue examples — readers want to know what to SAY
- Market data context — ground advice in current conditions
- Legal/compliance reminders — brief, not preachy: "always consult your broker"

## Topics That Consistently Sell
- First deal / getting started
- Lead generation systems
- Scripts for cold calling, expired listings, FSBOs
- Negotiation tactics
- Building a team
- Passive income through rentals
- House hacking
- The mindset shift from employee to entrepreneur

## Closing Chapter Must-Dos
- Real estate readers are action-oriented — end with momentum
- Give them a 30-day challenge or quick-start checklist
- Point them to a community, tool, or next resource (great for building your brand)
