# Business & Entrepreneurship Book Guide

## Reader Expectations
Business readers are busy. They want actionable frameworks, real-world proof, and clear takeaways they can use immediately. They have low tolerance for fluff and high expectations for credibility.

## Structure Patterns

### The Framework Book
Each chapter introduces one concept or framework, explains it, proves it with a case study, and ends with implementation steps. Think: *The E-Myth*, *Traction*, *Good to Great*.

### The Story-First Book
Opens with a compelling origin story, weaves narrative throughout, uses personal experience as the backbone. Think: *Shoe Dog*, *The Hard Thing About Hard Things*.

### The System Book
Teaches a repeatable system. Each chapter is a step in the process. The book functions like a manual the reader returns to. Think: *The 4-Hour Workweek*, *Buy Back Your Time*.

## Chapter Structure (recommended)
1. **Opening story** (300-500 words) — real example, ideally from the author or a recognizable figure
2. **The concept** — name it, define it, explain why it matters
3. **The framework** — present it visually if possible (describe a diagram, matrix, or checklist)
4. **Case study or proof** — show it working in practice
5. **Common mistakes** — what people get wrong
6. **Action step** — one concrete thing to do before the next chapter

## Voice Guidelines
- Write like you're talking to a smart peer, not lecturing
- Use "you" frequently — make it personal
- Short sentences for key insights; longer for context
- Jargon is OK if your audience knows it — but define on first use

## Credibility Markers
- Cite specific numbers and outcomes ("37% increase", "from $0 to $2M in 18 months")
- Name real companies and people where possible
- Acknowledge what doesn't work, not just what does

## Opening Chapter Must-Dos
- Establish the problem vividly — the reader should feel the pain
- Make a bold promise: here's what this book will do for you
- Give the reader a reason to trust you (briefly)

## Closing Chapter Must-Dos
- Synthesize the whole book into a single insight
- Paint the vision of what's possible if they implement
- Give a clear call to action (what to do first, today)
- For KDP/self-pub: ask for a review and point to next resource
